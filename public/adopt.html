Adopt a square meter of tropical forest!

At Green Conservation (the Foundation) we are deeply committed to preserving and safeguarding our precious forests for future generations. Forests play a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth, providing habitat for countless species, regulating the climate, and supplying vital resources that support human well-being. We are dedicated to advancing scientific understanding and conservation efforts for Earth's rich biodiversity. Our mission is to support cutting-edge research initiatives that explore, protect, and celebrate the incredible diversity of life on our planet.


By adopting a square meter of forest you become a vital partner in our efforts to conserve and restore the forests of Suriname. By joining forces with Green Conservation (the Foundation) you become a part of protecting our planet's most valuable ecosystems, in Suriname, South America.

Your financial support enables us to implement conservation projects on the ground and amplify our impact. 


Please do share our mission with your friends, family, and social networks to raise awareness and inspire others to join the cause.

Supporting the cause

Why forest conservation matters!

Forests are not just trees; they are complex ecosystems that support biodiversity and provide numerous ecosystem services essential for our survival. However, deforestation, habitat loss, and unsustainable land-use practices threaten the integrity of our forests at an alarming rate. By conserving and restoring these ecosystems, we can mitigate climate change, protect biodiversity, and secure a sustainable future for all.

How can you contribute to our goals?

By adopting a square meter of forest you become a vital partner in our efforts to conserve and restore the forests of Suriname. Your contribution directly funds initiatives such as:

 Reforestation Projects: Planting trees in degraded areas to restore habitat and sequester carbon.

 Biodiversity research activities: including but not limited to the initiation of a research facility, monitoring of wildlife species, documenting local species and their specific torpical habitats 

 Research and Advocacy: Supporting scientific research and advocating for policies that promote forest conservation and sustainable land management practices.

 Community Engagement: Empowering local communities to become stewards of their forests through education, training, and sustainable livelihood initiatives.

 Develop Conservation Strategies: Use scientific evidence to develop and implement conservation strategies that protect biodiversity and promote sustainable land and resource management.

 Selection, testing and Implementation of Green sustainable technologies.
